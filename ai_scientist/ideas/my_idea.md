# Title: Beyond Residual Connections: Structural Principles for Trainable Deep Networks

## Keywords
deep learning architectures, residual structures, trainability, robustness, network science

## TL;DR
Why are some deep neural architectures easier to train than others? This theme invites discussions on the structural principles behind trainable networks, with residual connections as a key starting point.

## Abstract
Residual connections have become a standard component of modern deep learning architectures, from ResNets and CNN variants to Transformers and large language models. Although they are often explained as a solution to vanishing or exploding gradients, this explanation does not fully account for their broad and persistent effectiveness across different model families, tasks, and scales. As deep learning systems continue to grow in size and complexity, there is an increasing need to understand not only whether an architecture works, but why certain structural designs make networks easier to optimize, more stable under perturbations, and more robust in practice.

This theme focuses on the structural principles underlying trainable deep networks. In particular, we are interested in residual structures and related architectural patterns that may improve learnability beyond conventional gradient-based explanations. Possible perspectives include network science, loss landscape analysis, perturbation robustness, random architecture design, modularity, shortcut pathways, and analogies to flexible complex systems such as brain networks or physical systems with frustration-like structures.

We invite contributions that investigate the mechanisms, empirical phenomena, and theoretical foundations of residual and non-residual architectures. Relevant topics may include, but are not limited to, why residual connections improve training, how architectural structures affect loss geometry, whether perturbation robustness can predict model performance, how CNNs, Transformers, and large language models differ in their reliance on residual pathways, and how structural insights can guide neural architecture search or future model design.

By bringing together researchers from deep learning, network science, optimization, statistical physics, neuroscience, and architecture search, this theme aims to build a broader discussion around the hidden structural factors that make deep networks trainable. The goal is not only to better understand residual connections, but also to identify general design principles for the next generation of robust, scalable, and efficient neural architectures.
